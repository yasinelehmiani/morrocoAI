Internal Regulations

The purpose of this internal regulations is to define the various categories of members of the association, as well as their roles, rights, and obligations. It aims to ensure a harmonious and transparent operation of the MoroccoAI association by governing the participation and commitment of its members.

Article 1: Categories of Members

The members of the association are divided into two main categories:

  • Individual Members:
    • Volunteers,
    • Active Members,
    • Prominent Members,
    • Honorary Members.
  • Legal Entity Members:
    • Companies and Institutions.

Article 2: Volunteers

Role: To contribute voluntarily to the activities of the association.

Membership Fee: No membership fee required.

Article 3: Members

Role: To have an interest in the association and align with the association's missions

Membership Fee:

  • Students: Payment of an annual fee of 300 MAD (30 dollars or euros)
  • Professionals: Payment of an annual fee of 500 MAD (50 dollars or euros)

Article 4: Active Members

Role: To actively participate in the association's initiatives, fully attend meetings, and contribute to projects. This member may be eligible for voting rights after submitting a request to the board of directors, subject to approval.

Membership Fee:

  • Students: Payment of an annual fee of 300 MAD (30 dollars or euros)
  • Professionals: Payment of an annual fee of 500 MAD (50 dollars or euros)

Article 5: Prominent Members

Role: To provide specific expertise, advise the association on specific subjects, and participate in key projects. This member may be eligible for voting rights after submitting a request to the board of directors, subject to approval.

Membership Fee: Exempt from membership fees, in recognition of their Prominent status.

Article 6: Honorary Members

Role: To be recognized for their exceptional contribution to the association, serve as ambassadors and role models. This member may be eligible for voting rights after submitting a request to the board of directors, subject to approval.

Membership Fee: Exempt from membership fees, in recognition of their honorary status.

Article 7: Companies and Institutions

Role: To financially and strategically support the activities of the association, collaborate on projects and partnerships aimed at promoting our common goals. Companies or institutions interested in voting rights must submit a request to the board of directors, which will be reviewed and approved subject to established criteria.

Membership Fee: Companies and institutions are invited to financially contribute to the association by paying an annual membership fee. The amount of the fee varies based on proposals provided by the association, with the following options:

  • 30000 MAD (3000 dollars or euros)
  • 50000 MAD (5000 dollars or euros)
  • 100000 MAD (10000 dollars or euros)

Article 8: Rights of Members

  • Members have the right to participate in activities and events organized by the association, in accordance with the provisions of the internal regulations.
  • Members have the right to submit proposals, ideas, or projects to the association for consideration and review by the board of directors.
  • Eligible members have the right to vote at the general meetings of the association, subject to the conditions and terms specified in the statutes and internal regulations.

Article 9: Obligations of Members

  • Members are required to comply with the statutes, internal regulations, and decisions made by the association's bodies.
  • Members must actively contribute to the achievement of the association's objectives and participate constructively in activities and projects.
  • Members are responsible for paying their annual membership fee within the deadlines specified by the association, in accordance with the established terms.

Article 10: Suspension or Expulsion of Members

Any member who seriously violates the statutes, internal regulations, or ethical principles of the association may be subject to temporary suspension or permanent expulsion, after review by the board of directors and motivated decision by the latter.
